Who We Are and What We’re Doing

Richmond Airlift is a small, community-driven effort to help our neighbors during a time of widespread uncertainty. You all know what we’re talking about.

For many people, leaving the house right now feels unsafe and scary. Our goal is to help neighbors stay in their homes while still getting everyday things done. This can include picking up groceries or prescriptions, getting supplies, walking dogs, shoveling snow, mowing grass, or helping with basic household tasks.

We intentionally keep groups small. If a group grows too large, this system is set up so new groups can be created to keep things safer and more manageable. If you think a new group is needed, reach out to an admin and they can help you figure out next steps.

In this project, neighbors are people requesting help. Helpers are people volunteering their time and effort to provide that help. We are doing this because our neighbors need support and we have the means to offer it.

What Helpers Are Signing Up For

Helpers communicate only through Signal. Signal is an end-to-end encrypted messaging app that limits data collection and helps protect everyone involved.

Signal can be used on iOS, Android, Windows, macOS, and Linux. Many helpers use it on both their phone and their computer.

We will try to communicate in English as much as possible. Many of our neighbors may not speak English, or may not speak it comfortably. It is a good idea to get familiar with a translation app on your phone so you can communicate clearly and respectfully when needed.

This project has a flat structure. That means helpers should be careful and thoughtful about who is added. Anyone added as a helper should be someone you trust completely. We all share responsibility for keeping our neighbors safe.

Helper Usernames and Identity Protection

To make helpers easy to identify and to protect neighbors, helpers use a consistent Signal username format.

  • Usernames should be the plus a name of your choosing
  • You may use your real name or a chosen name
  • Examples include theAlex, theRiver, theJordan

This helps neighbors quickly recognize who is a helper and reduces the risk of someone presenting themselves as a helper when they should not be. Everyone should stay alert and speak up if something feels off.

Getting Started With Signal

  1. Download Signal:
    • signal.org/download
    • Available on the Apple App Store, Google Play, and desktop
  2. Create your account using your phone number. This number does not need to be shared with others.
  3. Set your username by going to Settings, then Profile, then Username.
  4. In your privacy settings, enable options so people see your username and not your phone number.
  5. When sharing your contact link, generate the link inside the Signal app and use that link.

How the Signal Group Works

  1. A neighbor posts a request using a shared template. No identifying information is included at this stage.
  2. A helper reacts to the request with an emoji to show they are taking it.
  3. The helper starts a direct message with the neighbor to work out details privately.
  4. Only in the private message are specific details shared, such as an address or grocery list.
  5. The helper completes the task so the neighbor can stay safely at home.
